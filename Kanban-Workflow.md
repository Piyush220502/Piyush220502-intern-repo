**Agile Workflows & Kanban**

**Research and Learn**

How does a Kanban board work?

A Kanban board is a visual tool used to manage tasks and workflow.    
Tasks are represented as cards and move through different stages of work.    
It helps teams track progress, identify bottlenecks, and improve efficiency.

---

What do the columns represent?

Typical columns include:

\- Backlog: Tasks that are not started yet    
\- In Progress: Tasks currently being worked on    
\- Blocked: Tasks that are stuck or facing issues    
\- Done: Completed tasks  

These columns represent the different stages of a task in the workflow.

---

How do tasks move through the board?

Tasks move from left to right across columns as work progresses.    
The person working on the task is responsible for updating its status.    
This ensures transparency and keeps the team informed.

---

Benefits of limiting Work in Progress (WIP)

Limiting WIP helps:  
\- Avoid overload    
\- Improve focus    
\- Increase productivity    
\- Identify bottlenecks quickly  

---

**Reflection**

How does Kanban help manage priorities?

Kanban helps prioritize tasks by clearly showing what needs to be done next.    
It ensures that high-priority tasks are handled first and prevents task overload.

---

How can you improve workflow using Kanban?

Workflow can be improved by:  
\- Keeping tasks clearly defined    
\- Updating task status regularly    
\- Limiting work in progress    
\- Removing blockers quickly
