**Working in an Agile Team**

**Research and Learn**

What is Agile, and how does it differ from traditional project management?

Agile is an iterative and flexible approach to project management where work is completed in small increments and improved continuously based on feedback. It focuses on adaptability, collaboration, and delivering value quickly.

Traditional project management follows a fixed and linear approach where all requirements are defined at the beginning, and changes are difficult to implement later.

---

Core values and principles of Agile

Agile is based on four core values:

\- Individuals and interactions over processes and tools    
\- Working software over comprehensive documentation    
\- Customer collaboration over contract negotiation    
\- Responding to change over following a plan  

These values emphasize flexibility, teamwork, and continuous improvement.

---

Differences between Scrum and Kanban

Scrum is a structured framework that works in fixed time periods called sprints. It includes defined roles, ceremonies, and planning sessions.

Kanban is a continuous workflow system without fixed time frames. Tasks move through stages based on progress, and work is continuously updated.

---

Why Focus Bear uses Kanban instead of Scrum

Focus Bear prefers Kanban because it allows more flexibility. Tasks can be updated and prioritized continuously without waiting for a sprint cycle. It is more suitable for environments where work changes frequently.

**Reflection**

Benefits and challenges of Agile

Agile provides several benefits such as flexibility, faster delivery, improved communication, and continuous improvement. However, it can also present challenges such as unclear requirements, frequent changes, and the need for strong team coordination.

---

Agile helps me stay organized, adapt to changes quickly, and communicate effectively with the team. It encourages me to update my progress regularly and focus on delivering quality work.
