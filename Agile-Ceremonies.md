**Agile Ceremonies & Team Collaboration**

**Research and Learning**

What are the main Agile ceremonies and their purpose?

Daily Stand-up :-   
A short daily meeting where each team member shares:-  
\- What they worked on yesterday    
\- What they will work on today    
\- Any blockers or issues  

This helps the team stay aligned and quickly resolve problems.

Sprint Planning (Scrum) :-    
In Scrum, the team plans the work to be completed during a sprint. Tasks are selected from the backlog and assigned based on priority and capacity.

Continuous Prioritization (Kanban) :-    
Unlike Scrum, Kanban does not follow fixed sprints. Tasks are continuously updated and prioritized based on current needs.

Retrospective :-    
A meeting held after a sprint where the team reflects on:  
\- What went well    
\- What did not go well    
\- What improvements can be made  

Backlog Refinement :-    
The process of reviewing and organizing upcoming tasks to ensure they are clear, well-defined, and prioritized.

How do Agile teams collaborate asynchronously and across time zones?

Agile teams use various tools and practices to collaborate effectively:  
\- GitHub for managing tasks, issues, and code    
\- Communication tools like Slack or Microsoft Teams    
\- Clear documentation to share updates and instructions  

Asynchronous communication allows team members to contribute without needing to be online at the same time, making collaboration flexible and efficient.

**Reflection**  
How do Agile ceremonies help with communication and alignment?

Agile ceremonies improve communication by keeping everyone informed about progress and responsibilities.    
Daily stand-ups ensure regular updates, sprint planning aligns the team on goals, and retrospectives encourage continuous improvement.    
These practices help maintain clarity, reduce misunderstandings, and ensure everyone is working toward the same objectives.
