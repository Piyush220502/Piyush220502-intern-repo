**Roles in an Agile Team**

**Research and Learn**

What are the key roles in an Agile team, and what does each role do?

Developers:  
Developers are responsible for writing code, building features, and implementing the product requirements. They ensure that the product is functional and meets technical standards.

QA (Quality Assurance):   
QA engineers test the product to identify bugs and ensure quality. They verify that the application works correctly and meets user expectations.

Product Manager (PM):   
The Product Manager defines the product vision and priorities. They manage the backlog and ensure that the team is working on the most important tasks.

Agile Project Manager (Scrum Master):  
The Agile Project Manager or Scrum Master facilitates the Agile process. They remove blockers, ensure smooth communication, and help the team follow Agile practices.

Other Supporting Roles:    
These include stakeholders, UX designers, and customer support teams. They contribute by providing feedback, improving user experience, and supporting users.

---

How do these roles collaborate in Kanban vs. Scrum?

In Scrum, collaboration happens through structured ceremonies such as sprint planning, daily stand-ups, and retrospectives. Each role has defined responsibilities and works together during each sprint.

In Kanban, collaboration is more flexible and continuous. Tasks move through the workflow without fixed sprints, and team members update progress regularly. Communication is more asynchronous and ongoing.
