**Cyber Security Guidelines**

**Research and Learn**

Common cyber security threats in a remote work environment

Common cyber security threats include phishing emails, weak passwords, malware, suspicious links, unsecured Wi-Fi networks, and unauthorized access to accounts or devices. Remote work environments are more vulnerable because employees access company systems from different locations and devices.

---

Best practices for keeping devices and accounts secure

Some important best practices include:  
\- Using strong and unique passwords    
\- Enabling two-factor authentication (2FA)    
\- Keeping software and devices updated    
\- Avoiding suspicious links and downloads    
\- Using secure internet connections    
\- Locking devices when away from the desk  

These practices help reduce security risks and protect sensitive information.

---

Importance of locking your computer

Locking your computer prevents unauthorized access when you are away from your workspace. It helps protect company data, personal information, and work accounts from misuse.

---

Handling phishing attempts and suspicious links

Phishing attempts should be handled carefully by avoiding unknown links, attachments, or suspicious messages. Before clicking any link, it is important to verify the sender and check the authenticity of the email or website.

---

Strong passwords and password managers

A strong password should contain a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Password managers help securely store and manage passwords without needing to remember every password manually.

---

Importance of two-factor authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds an additional layer of security by requiring a second verification step besides the password. It should be enabled on important accounts such as email, GitHub, and work-related platforms.
